How SATFARM works

SATFARM is a hub that coordinates SAT jobs, workers, and verification. You have a single account; from your dashboard you mint client and worker tokens and point them at your own machines, clusters, or lab servers.

Step 1

Create an account & issue tokens

Sign in once, then generate API tokens. Use them in satfarm-client to post jobs and in satfarm-worker to rent out compute—no separate identities needed.

Step 2

Run SAT nodes on your own hardware

Point worker nodes at SATFARM using your worker token. They receive jobs, run solvers like kissat, and upload proofs and models back to the hub.

Step 3

Verify, dispute, and settle

Clients verify locally or via SATFARM’s checkers. Disputes invoke an on-platform replay using independent tools; stakes and reputation enforce honest behavior.

Built for verifiable, incentive-aligned SAT solving

Proof-first design

Every UNSAT result is backed by DRAT/GRAT certificates; every SAT result by a model. No opaque “solver says so” answers—only verifiable artifacts.

One identity, many nodes

A single account can back multiple client and worker nodes. Spin up transient workers on cloud VMs, keep long-lived ones on-prem, all linked to the same balance and reputation.

Trustless matching

Clients and workers never have to trust each other. The hub coordinates jobs, holds stakes, and uses proofs plus independent checkers to resolve disagreements.

Pluggable solvers & checkers

Use your preferred SAT solvers and proof checkers, or rely on a standard stack (e.g. kissat + gratgen + gratchk). SATFARM records which toolchain was used per job.

Auditable job history

Every job keeps a trail: who solved it, which artifacts were submitted, and how verification and disputes were resolved. Great for research reproducibility.

Reputation with teeth

Honest behavior compounds. Repeated bogus proofs or complaints erode reputation and can trigger higher staking requirements or reduced job visibility.

For people with problems and people with cores

SATFARM is meant to be neutral ground: a place where compute-rich users and problem-rich users can meet safely. Accounts are symmetric—you decide from the dashboard whether a given token behaves as a client, a worker, or both.
